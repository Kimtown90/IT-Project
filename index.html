Project Ideas

My project idea is straightforward yet potentially very impactful on our daily lives. My focus is to create
and develop a mobile application that is used to find precisely where we store our personal belongings, mementos
, family heirlooms and any other everyday household item. The concept is simple:
1) Input an item into an index.
2) Leave a description of where you are stowing it and have that cached into a database for future use.
3) When the time comes and you need to find this item, simply search for the item in the index and the items location appears.

Typically, I am a forgetful person by nature and this application is for those people out there, like me, who
need aid in finding their possessions. This app has the potential to shed copious amounts of time for those
who are continuously searching for their misplaced personal property. Pixie’s research at apartmenttherapy.com
reveals that we spend on average a whopping 2.5 days a year searching for our belongings. That is around 5000
hours of our entire lives wasted. Not only is this insanely time consuming, it often frustratingly leads to
missing important deadlines, such as making appointments on time, missing transport in particularly flights or
trains trips, and even just every day activities such as arriving for work or school on time. The financial
implications of misplacing items also add up. With the help of this app, no longer will its users have to replace
expensive commodities they have lost throughout their lives.


Figure 4. Statistics on the biggest consequences for losing items according to Apartment Therapy

This application will be very simple to use and anyone with rudimentary app experince can use it effectively.
Initially, the user will list their physical belongings they wish to store away, in a database with the
location of exactly where they wish to store it. Lets use a passport for an example. I want to deposit this
in a secure location like the top drawer on the left side of my cabinet in my study area for future use.
I open the application, Add in “Passport”, followed by something along the lines of “Top, Left Drawer in
Study” in a description field. When the time comes in the future to find this Passport, simply enter
“Passport” into the search bar and the description of the location will appear. Simple. This could also
include a potential paid subscription for a Cloud type storage database so belongings are backed up online,
rather than just on the physical memory of the device alone.

It could also incorporate a GPS function for the position of each item, if items are stored away in locations
away from your own property. This will be useful for people who have personal belongings in work buildings,
storage units, containers, garages or warehouses in separate locations. The same principles of adding in the
item apply. Input “Passport”, input “Add current GPS Waypoint” and then possibly a further detailed description
of exactly where that item is at that GPS waypoint, e.g: “Office Desk Filing Cabinet under P”.
Another potential feature may be an alert or reminder system for the items stored away. For example, if you were
storing away important documents for future use, you could set a reminder for that specific document for when
you need to locate it and the app will alert you exactly where it is, when you need it at that point in time.

The overall overlay of the application will be as user friendly as possible. An uncomplicated home screen with
the options to Input Items or Search Items, both being clickable which will then take you into the respective
fields. Utilizing voice recognition on the device, and also Speech-to-Text functions could also be implemented
to save time and effort in having to enter in categories manually by typing. There is also potential to pairing
GPS tracking devices to the app as well. Physically attaching a tracking device to a certain object, be it car
keys, wallet or even a separate phone and have the exact GPS location displayed within the app could also be
implemented.

My aim is to create a mobile application that is usable for both iOS and Android operating systems.
According to statcounter.com, these two operating systems make up just shy of 100% of the global market share.
As of August 2020, iOS is responsible for 25.15% of the market share while Android has an incredible 74.25% share.
Having the application available for both operating systems allows the largest target audience possible.
I plan to develop this application on my own personal PC, running windows 10. I have narrowed down the
potential software used to Xcode, Java and VirtualBox, however further research and physical testing into
each of these will be required before the product comes into fruition. Xcode is developed for Mac only,
so I would need an additional program to enable this, such as MacStadium, to be able to run this on my Windows PC.
This is what I would likely use to create the iOS version of the app. Whereas Java or VirtualBox would be used to
develop the Android version.

Through my RMIT studies, I intend to build my knowledge in an array of different coding languages, not only for
use with this application but also for my own personal knowledge. Java and C++ tend to be the most popular for

A successful app for finding key personal belongings will play a major factor in helping us live our lives more
